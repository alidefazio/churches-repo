Three Years

The Daesh's Destruction of Qaraqosh

When Father Abona Ammar,

an Assyrian Catholic priest, left his hometown of Qaraqosh, Iraq five years ago he took nothing with him. He was told the were invading. “My thought was maybe we leave for few hours, maybe one day, maybe two days. Not more,” Ammar said.

“We couldn’t imagine that it would be for three years,” he said.

In those three years, churches in Qaraqosh, Iraq’s largest Christian town, were destroyed and looted by the Daesh. They leveled a 700 year old and turned a 6th century church into a weapons The largest church, Al Tahira (The Great Immaculate Church), was burned from the inside out and is still unusable to this day.

“You know when someone loses everything, how can you feel? You lose your city, your home, your church,” said Ammar. “How can you feel?”

Using open source investigative techniques and satellite imagery, we confirmed the locations of Christian churches damaged or destroyed by the Daesh in Qaraqosh.

In total, we confirmed the locations of 10 of Qaraqosh's major Christian churches.
