When Father Abona Ammar, an Assyrian Catholic priest, left his hometown of Qaraqosh, Iraq five years ago he took nothing with him. He was told the Daesh were invading. “My thought was maybe we leave for few hours, maybe one day, maybe two days. Not more,” Ammar said.

“We couldn’t imagine that it would be for three years,” he said.

In those three years, churches in Qaraqosh, Iraq’s largest Christian town, were destroyed and looted by the Daesh. They leveled a 700 year old monastery and turned a 6th century church into a weapons factory. The largest church, Al Tahira (The Great Immaculate Church), was burned from the inside out and is still unusable to this day.

“You know when someone loses everything, how can you feel? You lose your city, your home, your church,” said Ammar. “It was a great, great pain.”
